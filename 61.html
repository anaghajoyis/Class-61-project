Here are a few fun facts about them!
1. While us humans have 206 bones, cats on average have 244. It ranges between 230-250 depending on how long a cat’s tail is and how many toes the cat has.
2. Adult cats have 30 teeth, while kittens have 26
3. A house cat is genetically 95.6% tiger.
4. Cats can run around 48 kph (30 mph), but only over short distances. A house cat could beat superstar runner Usain Bolt in the 200 meter dash.
5. Cats can jump 5 times their height
6. An adult cat’s brain is approximately 5 cm (2 inches) long and weighs 30g and has nearly twice the amount of neurons in their cerebral cortex as dogs.
7. Males are more likely to be left pawed, while females and more likely to be right pawed- some are ambidextrous.
8. A cat’s tongue contains a number of backward facing hooks known as filiform papillae, these rough tongues can lick bones clean of any bits of meat.
9. Cats have an extra organ that allows them to taste scents in the air
10. Each cat’s nose is unique, much like human fingerprints.
11. Neutered males live 62% longer than unneutered cats, and spayed females live 39% longer than unspayed females
12. Between 2002 and 2012 the average lifespan of a cat increased by a year
13. Cat whiskers are the same width as their body.
14. Cats walk like camels and giraffes, both right feet then both left feet.
15. Cats sleep 12-16 hours per day, and spend 1/3 of their awake hours grooming
16. Cats can dream just like humans
17. Grooming stimulates blood flow to the skin, regulates body temperature, and helps them relax.
18. Cats mark you as their territory by rubbing their faces and bodies against you, marking you with their scent
19. If your cat approaches you with a straight, almost vibrating tail, this means that he/she is extremely happy to see you
20. When your cat shows their belly, it is a sign of trust and a relaxed cat- this is not an invite for belly rub typically.
21. A cat’s rear end in your face is a gesture of friendship, while kneading means happiness. Draping their tail over another cat/dog/you means friendship also.
22. Meowing is a behavior cats developed to communicate with humans, and they can have up to 100 different vocalizations. A cat’s purr vibrates at a frequency of 25 to 150 hertz, which is the same frequency at which muscles and bones repair themselves.
23. Grimalkin is the name of a female cat, especially an older one.
24. An ailurophile is a person who loves cats. The word ailuro is the ancient Greek word for cat.
25. There are about 88 million pet cats in the United States, which makes them the most popular pet in the country.

My Inspirations!

Kalpana Chawla flew only 2 missions to space. Her first mission began on November 19th 1997. During her first mission, she did all her work and returned back safely. Her second mission began on January 16th 2003. On 1st of Febuary 2003, NASA's Space shuttle Columbia exploded while reentering Earth's atmosphere, killing all its seven crew members including Kalpana Chawla, the first Indian-origin woman to go to space. The space shuttle was destroyed at about 7:30 PM IST on February 1, 2003, after a 16-day scientific mission. Rick Husband, Micheal Anderson, David Brown, Kalpana Chawla, Laurel Clark, William McCool and Ilan Ramon were the 7 crew members in the Columbia Space shuttle. Rick Husband was the Commander. Micheal Anderson was the Payload Commander. David Brown was the Mission Specialist. Kalpana Chawla was the Mission Specialist. Laurel Clark was the Mission Specialist. William McCool was the pilot. Ilan Ramon was the Payload Specialist. A part of Columbia broke up when it neared the Earth’s surface, which caused a problem while returning. This caused to much heat inside the ship, which cause all the crew members to die. After she dies, one of her brothers said “ She is in a place she is meant to be, among the stars” Kalpana Chawla was India’s first woman astronaut. She was born in Karnal in India on July 1, 1961 to a poor family. She was the youngest of the the 4 children. The name ‘Kalpana’ means imagination. Her brothers and sisters called her ‘Montu’ because she was small. Right from when she was small, her dream was to fly to Space. She studied in Tagore Baal Niketan Senior Secondary School, Karnal and her B.Tech in Aeronautical Engineering from Punjab Engineering College at Chandigarh, India in 1982. She was the only girl in Aeronautical Engineering because then girls in India were not allowed go outside and study. From her life, we learnt that we need to work hard inspite of the difficulties and need to achieve in such a way that everyone will remember us even if we die just like Kalpana Chawla and she is also a great inspiration to children like me... She is my inspiration to start loving astronomy, this is because she showed me that the family wealth, background, religion etc don't matter if you really want to achieve something. She also showed me that we should never give up no matter what happens
